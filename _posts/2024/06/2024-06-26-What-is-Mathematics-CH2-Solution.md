## Incommensurable segments, irrational numbers, and the concept of limit

1. Prove that $$ \sqrt{2}, \sqrt{3}, \sqrt{5}, \sqrt{6} $$ are not rational. (Hint: Use the lemma of p. 47).

2. Prove that $$ \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3} $$ and $$ \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{5} $$ are not rational. (Hint: If e.g. the first of these numbers were equal to a rational number \( r \), then, writing $$ \sqrt{3} = r - \sqrt{2} $$ and squaring, $$ \sqrt{2} $$ would be rational.)

3. Prove that $$ \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{5} $$ is irrational. Try to make up similar and more general examples.
